Privacy Policy

How we collect and use your information — written in plain English, not legalese.

1. Who we are

We're H E Tyres Ltd — a family-run tyre fitting business based in Swindon. We offer mobile tyre fitting across the local area and a depot service at Unit 5 Star West, Westmead Drive, Westlea, Swindon SN5 7SW.

For the purposes of UK data protection law, H E Tyres Ltd is the data controller for personal information collected through this website. That means we decide what we collect and why, and we're responsible for keeping it safe.

2. What we collect and why

We only collect information that helps us do the job — giving you a quote or getting your tyres fitted. Bookings themselves are made by phone, not through the website, so we don't collect or store any personal information when you book. Here's what we collect and the reason for it.

A few of the features described below — such as vehicle look-up and postcode/address look-up — are optional add-ons that we can switch on or off. If a "Look up" or "Find address" button isn't showing on the site when you visit, that feature isn't currently switched on and the related third party isn't receiving your information. We've kept them described here so this policy stays accurate whenever we do turn them on.

2a. Enquiry form

When you fill in the "Mobile fitting or other enquiry" form on our site, we collect:

Information Why we collect it
Your name So we know who we're talking to when we call back
Your phone number To call or text you with a price or to confirm a mobile visit
Your email address (optional) To send a follow-up if we can't reach you by phone
Fitting postcode (mobile enquiries) To confirm we cover your area and plan the visit — see section 2e if the address finder is switched on
Vehicle registration (optional) To look up your tyre size and vehicle details so we can price the job accurately — see section 2b
Tyre size, tyre count, timing preference (mobile enquiries, optional) To prepare a quote and plan the job
Free-text message (general enquiries) To understand what you need and give you a useful answer

We collect this information to respond to your enquiry and provide the service you've asked about. The legal basis is legitimate interests — both yours (getting a quick, useful response) and ours (running our business).

Your enquiry is sent to us by email. We don't store it in a separate database — it lives in our inbox and is deleted when the conversation is complete or when it's no longer needed.

2b. Vehicle registration lookup (enquiry form) — optional feature

Our enquiry form can include a registration look-up ("Look up") next to the vehicle registration field. Where this feature is switched on and you use it, your registration is sent to one of our vehicle data providers — currently RegCheck (regcheck.org.uk) or VehicleDataGlobal.com, both UK vehicle data services. They return details such as your vehicle's make, model, colour, year, fuel type and, where available, tyre size.

We use this to pre-fill your tyre size where possible and to include the vehicle details in your enquiry so we can quote accurately. The registration and the details returned are included in the enquiry email sent to us — they're not stored separately.

The legal basis is legitimate interests — using your reg to get your vehicle details saves both parties time and reduces quoting errors. Using the lookup is optional; you can leave the registration field blank or type it in without clicking "Look up". If the "Look up" button isn't shown on the form, this feature isn't currently switched on and your registration isn't sent to either provider.

RegCheck's own privacy information is at regcheck.org.uk; VehicleDataGlobal's is at vehicledataglobal.com.

2c. Interactive maps

Our site includes two interactive maps — one showing our coverage area and one showing directions to the depot. These maps use Stadia Maps for the map tiles (the background images that make up the map). When the maps load, your browser makes a request to Stadia Maps' servers, which means your IP address is sent to them as part of that request.

We don't deliberately share any personal information with Stadia Maps — an IP address is sent as a side effect of how the web works. The maps only load when you scroll down to them. Stadia Maps' privacy policy is at stadiamaps.com/privacy-policy.

2d. Analytics cookies (opt-in)

We use Google Analytics to understand how people find and use our website — for example, which pages are most visited and where visitors come from. This helps us improve the site.

Google Analytics is switched off by default. It only activates if you choose "Accept all" on the cookie banner when you first visit. If you decline, no analytics data is collected at all.

If you accept, Google Analytics sets cookies on your device and collects anonymised information such as pages visited, time spent, and a rough geographic area (city level). Your IP address is anonymised. No information that identifies you personally is shared with Google.

The legal basis is consent — you can withdraw your consent at any time by clicking "Manage cookie preferences" in the footer, or by clearing your cookies.

Google's privacy policy is at policies.google.com/privacy.

2e. Postcode and address lookup (optional feature)

Our enquiry form can include a "Find address" feature alongside the fitting postcode field, for mobile fitting enquiries. This feature is not currently switched on. Where it is switched on, entering your postcode and tapping "Find address" sends your postcode to Postcoder, a UK address-lookup service, which returns a list of addresses at that postcode for you to pick from — saving you typing your full address and reducing errors in the address we're given.

The legal basis is legitimate interests — making the form quicker and more accurate for both parties. Using the address finder is optional; you can always type your postcode and address in by hand. If the "Find address" button isn't shown on the form, we're not using this feature and your postcode isn't sent to Postcoder.

Postcoder's privacy information is at postcoder.com/privacy-policy.

5. Your rights

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, you have a number of rights over your personal information:

Access

You can ask us for a copy of any personal information we hold about you.

Correction

You can ask us to correct any information we have that's wrong or out of date.

Deletion

You can ask us to delete your information in certain circumstances — for example, if you've withdrawn consent or we no longer need it.

Objection

You can object to us using your information where the legal basis is legitimate interests. We'll stop unless we have a compelling reason to continue.

Restriction

You can ask us to limit how we use your information while a dispute is resolved.

Withdraw consent

Where we rely on your consent (analytics cookies), you can withdraw it at any time. This doesn't affect anything that's already happened.

To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details in section 6. We'll respond within one month.

If you're not happy with how we handle your request — or with our use of your data generally — you have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO). You can contact them at ico.org.uk or on 0303 123 1113. We'd always prefer to hear from you first so we can try to resolve things directly.

6. Cookies

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. We use two types:

  • Essential cookies — these are needed for the site to work correctly (for example, remembering your cookie preference). You can't turn these off, but they don't track you.
  • Analytics cookies — Google Analytics cookies (_ga, _ga_*). These are switched off by default and only set if you actively choose to accept them.

You can manage or change your cookie preference at any time by clicking "Manage cookie preferences" in the site footer.

You can also delete cookies at any time through your browser settings. Most browsers let you block or delete cookies — check your browser's help pages for instructions.
