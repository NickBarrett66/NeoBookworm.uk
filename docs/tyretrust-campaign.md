# TyreTrust — Direct-Mail Campaign Pack

Brand: **TyreTrust** — standalone ("websites built only for tyre fitters").
Audience: ~29 local tyre fitters, ~50-mile radius of Swindon (Wilts, Glos, Bath/Bristol,
Oxon, Berks, N. Hants). Many are sole traders — **you can post to them, and legally phone
them for follow-up**, which cold email never allowed.
Landing page: **tyretrust.uk** (built — `tyretrust/index.html`), HE Tyres as the live worked example.
Model: £0 to build, *pay what you think it's worth*, then £9.99/mo core + à-la-carte add-ons.
Goal: a steady stack of small recurring fees across many clients, not big one-offs.

---

## Why this replaces the email spray

| Cold email (what failed) | Direct mail to this cohort (what should work) |
|---|---|
| Invisible — they're under the ramp, not at a desk | Physical, rare, tactile — it gets picked up |
| "Another web person" — instant delete | You already **built the thing** — proof, not pitch |
| One touch, no legal follow-up to sole traders | Letter → **phone call** → optional 2nd postcard |
| Ltd-only (many past the point of needing you) | Sole traders: hungrier, earlier, un-sold-to |

The one move that works: **"I built a real one for HE Tyres up the road. Go and use it.
Yours could look the same. Free to look."** Reciprocity + proof + zero risk.

---

## The campaign sequence (per prospect)

1. **Letter** (below) — personalised with business name + town, QR to tyretrust.uk.
2. **~7 days later: phone call** — the warm nudge (script below). This is the step email never had.
3. **~3 weeks: optional postcard** to non-responders — one image, the QR, one line.
4. **On interest** → build/adjust their site → send link → they pay what they think + £9.99/mo.

### Two-tier effort (matches "demo page for all + bespoke for best few")
- **Tier 1 — top ~5 prospects:** pre-build a bespoke site (HE Tyres treatment) *before* posting,
  and change the letter's key line to *"So I built you one — take a look: tyretrust.uk/theirtown."*
  Highest conversion. Pick the 5 with the most to gain: no site at all, decent Facebook reviews,
  clearly active, ideally two named owners.
- **Tier 2 — the other ~24:** the standard letter drives them to **tyretrust.uk** (HE Tyres as the
  example) + the "I'll build yours free to look" offer. Only build after they show interest.

## PHONE FOLLOW-UP SCRIPT (~1 week after the letter)

Sole traders can be called. Keep it 30 seconds, low-pressure, no "sales voice."

> "Hi, is that **[name]**? It's Nick — I sent you a letter last week about tyre-fitter
> websites, I'm the chap over in Swindon. I built one for HE Tyres and I mentioned you could
> have a look at a real one online. Did the letter reach you okay? … No worries if you've not
> had a chance — if you get two minutes, have a look at **tyretrust.uk** on your phone and
> book a pretend slot; it'll make more sense than me talking at you. If you like it I'll build
> yours free to look, and you only pay if you're happy with it. Shall I give you a shout again
> next week, or is it not your sort of thing?"

**If interested:** get business name, town, depot/mobile/both, and whether they've a domain.
Then build (or adjust the pre-built) and send the link.
**If "not interested":** "No problem at all — if you ever change your mind it's tyretrust.uk.
Cheers, take care." (Leaves the door open, costs nothing.)

## Practical notes & ideas

- **Print quality = trust.** Properly printed letter on decent stock, real signature, real QR.
  Cheap laser-print + mail-merge "Dear Business Owner" reads as scam. This cohort is allergic to that.
- **Address both owners** where you can (the Emma-and-Howard effect — the admin partner is
  usually the one who champions a website).
- **QR → tyretrust.uk**, which itself has a *live booking demo* button. Let them feel it in
  the workshop in 20 seconds — that's the moment that converts.
- **Referral hook (later):** offer existing clients a free month for introducing another fitter.
  Fitters know each other locally; TyreTrust's niche makes word-of-mouth travel inside the trade.
- **Stack, don't bundle:** land them on £9.99, add £6.99 booking a month later once they've felt
  the saved calls. Every individual "yes" is a small, easy number — which is exactly your
  steady-recurring goal.
- **HE Tyres permission:** the whole page + letters lean on HE Tyres as the reference. Confirm
  Emma & Howard are happy to be named/used as an example (and potentially fielding a call from a
  curious fitter). The page is built so HE Tyres is a swap-out if they decline.
- **Domain:** register **tyretrust.uk** (and grab tyretrust.co.uk) before posting — the QR and
  letter both point at it.
