# TyreTrust letter pack — Smart Tyres Gloucester (Jiri)

Per-prospect direct-mail sequence for the Tier-1 **pre-built** approach (site built
before posting). Speed-first / walk-in positioning = TyreTrust's **"The Site"**
plan (£9.99/mo, pay-what-you-think build). Deliberately does **not** sell the
booking engine, but leaves the door open to the booking version in case the
walk-in read is wrong.

**Sequence:** one A4 sheet printed both sides (letter + proof), then two A6
postcard chase-ups. Amalgamates the Gemini one-pager's walk-in/speed insight
with the Opus 3-touch campaign's voice, envelope discipline and scarcity
reconciliation (July 2026).

- **Brand:** TyreTrust throughout, `nick@tyretrust.uk` (assumes the domain +
  mailbox exist before posting — pre-flight blocker).
- **Owner:** Jiri (confirmed via Companies House — Ltd company director).
- **Reviews (verified by Nick online, 11 Jul 2026):** Google **4.9★ / 184
  reviews** · Facebook **98% recommend / 30 reviews**. Letter, site and schema
  all now use these figures — keep them in sync.
- **Response path:** QR / short URL → the **personalised TyreTrust landing page**
  (`?p=smart-tyres-gloucester`, built + site-focus mode) — his site sits in the
  hero with "See your site →", pricing and the lead form (with the built-mode
  "Anything you'd change?" box) are one scroll down. NOT the plain site directly
  (it carries no TyreTrust contact — a dead end), and NOT the booking variant
  (`/smart-tyres-gloucester-booking/` is only for the "if I've misjudged it"
  follow-up).

## Pre-flight checklist (lock before anything prints)

1. **QR target = the merge URL** for `smart-tyres-gloucester` from
   [docs/tyretrust-mailshot-codes.csv](tyretrust-mailshot-codes.csv) (landing
   page + `?p=` + UTM). Test-scan a printed proof end-to-end: banner greets
   Jiri, hero shows his site, "See your site →" opens `/smart-tyres-gloucester/`,
   The Site card is the featured plan.
2. **`tyretrust.uk` domain + mailbox live** (Nick is purchasing) so the
   letterhead/sign-off address works, **plus the short-URL route**
   `tyretrust.uk/smart-tyres-gloucester` → merge URL. Roughly a third of
   readers type rather than scan — the long URL is not typeable. Switch the
   landing page's mailto/footer/canonical from `neobookworm.uk` addresses at
   the same time, so paper and page show the same email.
3. **Real review quotes on the site.** Counts are now correct (Google 4.9/184,
   Facebook 98%/30), but the six named review cards on
   `/smart-tyres-gloucester/` are placeholders. Replace with genuine quoted
   reviews (real names/dates) before mailing — the letter's opening line is
   "your reviews", so the reviews are the first thing Jiri will check.
4. **Price wording:** letter and landing pages match — "pay what you think
   it's worth; as a guide £150–£300; pay less if money's tight or you don't
   think it's worth that." Keep them in sync if either changes.
5. **Envelope:** plain white C5, stamped (not franked), hand-addressed style,
   no window, no logo on the front — per the campaign's highest-leverage rule
   in `docs/tyretrust-mailshot-copy.md`.
6. **Timing:** aim for Tuesday–Thursday arrival. Never Saturday (his busiest
   day, 9:00–4:30). Don't judge the campaign before day 60.

## TOUCH 1 — A4 sheet, printed both sides (day 0)

### Side 1 — the letter

**TyreTrust**
Websites built only for tyre fitters
nick@tyretrust.uk
{date}

Dear Jiri,

Your reviews all say the same thing — in and out fast, tyres done properly, no messing about. 184 of them, sitting at 4.9 stars. That reputation is exactly why I've done something slightly unusual: I've already built you a website, before writing to you.

I'm Nick, and I build websites only for independent tyre businesses. I built yours around how I think you actually work — walk-ins and fast turnarounds. The big chains push complicated booking diaries because their logistics are slow; a depot like yours wins by being ready on the spot. So there's no diary to manage and nothing added to your day. Your site does the three things that actually send a driver to Hempsted Lane:

- Turns up the second someone searches "tyres near me" on their phone
- Puts your 4.9 stars and your part-worn stock front and centre
- One tap to call you, one tap for directions to the depot

Here's the deal, and I've made it as easy to say yes to as I know how:

**It's already built.** Turn this page over, point your phone camera at the code, and look it over right now. Your site's at the top of the page it opens, with what it costs underneath. If it's not for you, close the tab — no bill, no obligation.

**You set the price.** If you want to keep it, you decide what the build was worth. As a guide, £150–£300 is a fair starting point — but pay less if money's tight, or if you honestly don't think it's worth that. I won't argue. To keep it live on your own web address, it's £9.99 a month, cancel anytime, and the address and the files are yours to walk away with.

Regards,
Nick Barrett
TyreTrust, Swindon

*P.S. There's nobody else here — no sales team, no commission, no follow-up call from someone who isn't me. The site's built, it's live, and if it's not for you, that's genuinely the end of it.*
