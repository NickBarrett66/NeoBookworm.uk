# TyreTrust — Mailshot Copy (3-touch sequence)

Written Jul 2026. Audience: 29 tyre fitters, ~50 miles of Swindon, no website, mostly sole traders.
Voice rules: `docs/neobookworms-voice-tyreTrust.md`. Never criticise the reader. First person singular.
Plain English. No "professional", "modern", "solution", "digital", "presence".

Merge fields available per prospect: `{{Business}}`, `{{Town}}`, `{{MergeURL}}` (QR), `{{ShortURL}}`.

---

## Envelope (Touch 1) — the most important decision on this page

- Plain white C5. **No window. No logo on the front. No franking mark.**
- Real stamp, 2nd class. Stannp supports stamped mail — pay for it.
- Address in a handwriting font, slightly off-square.
- Back flap only, small, 8pt: `TyreTrust · Swindon`

A franked window envelope with a logo is a bill or an ad. It gets binned unopened and none of the
copy below ever gets read.

> **TyreTrust**

> calendar while his hands are full. The customer gets a confirmation, then a reminder
> the night before, and if they need to move it they move it themselves — the slot
> fills again instead of sitting empty. No phone ringing while he's got a wheel off.

> That's the thing I've built, and I'd like a second business running it for real.
>
> **So here's my offer, and I've made it as easy to say yes to as I know how.**

### Side 2 — the proof

**Two images, telling the two ends of one job.** Both are real, both are shot at HE Tyres,
neither is a rendered mockup and neither is stock.

**Main image, top half — the fitter's end.** A phone or tablet propped on a workbench in the
bay, showing the day view: today's jobs, whose tyres have arrived, what's still waiting on
stock. Wheel off in the background, tools in shot, screen slightly grubby. Nobody has to be
holding it — the point is that it sits there working while both hands are busy.

**Small inset, bottom-left of the main image — the customer's end.** A phone on a kitchen
table or a car dashboard, showing the booking screen mid-flow. Different place, different

## TOUCH 3 — half-page letter, day +40

The last one. Short, no pitch, and this is the only piece that hints at what comes next.

> **{{Business}}**
>
> This is the last thing I'll post you — three was always the plan, and I'd rather stop
> than become the bloke who won't take a hint.
>
> Two things worth saying before I do.
>
> The first is about the price, because I've been vague and you deserve better than vague.
> *Pay what you think it's worth* isn't a permanent offer and it was never meant to be. It
> exists because I've one tyre customer and I need a handful of sites out in the world doing
> their job, where fitters can see them. Once I've got those, I'll charge properly for a
> build, because by then I'll have something to point at. I can't tell you what date that
> is. It could be next month. It depends entirely on who says yes.
>
> What doesn't run out is me. If this is the wrong month — you're flat out, the van's off
> the road, it's just not the time — then email me whenever it is the right month and I'll
> still build you a site. It may cost you a fixed price by then rather than whatever you
> thought it was worth. But I'll still be here, and I'll still pick up.
>
> The second thing is that the site and the booking are the start of it, not the end. The plate
> already tells the booking which car is coming. The piece I'm finishing now goes further
> — it comes back with what that car actually wears: the size front and rear, the load and
> speed ratings, whether it's run-flat, what the pressures should be. Your customer books
> knowing, and you know what's coming before the car does.
>
> I'm not selling you that today. I'm telling you it exists so you know this thing has
> somewhere to go, and that I'm still here building it.
>
> If you ever want to look, the code below still works and it still knows who you are.
>
> Regards,
>
> *Nick*
>
> nick@tyretrust.uk · TyreTrust, Swindon
>
> [QR — {{MergeURL}}]  **{{ShortURL}}**

**Image:** the reg look-up mid-lookup — plate typed in, tyre sizes and pressures coming back —
small, right-hand side, greyscale so it reads as a glimpse rather than an offer.

⚠️ **Do not print touch 3 until this screen exists.** The VDG tyre lookup is written
(`api/_lib/vdg.js`) and proven against `reg-test.html`, but it is not wired into the booking
widget and it is on a trial key. Before this letter goes to press: port the lookup to
`worker/routes/`, buy a real `VDG_API_KEY`, switch it on for one tenant, screenshot the real
thing. You have ~40 days from touch 1 to do it, which is plenty — but a greyscale glimpse of
a screen that doesn't exist is the one lie in this campaign that a prospect could actually
catch you in.

## Timing

| Touch | Day | Piece |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | A4 letter, C5 stamped envelope |
| 2 | +18 | A6 postcard |
| 3 | +40 | Half-page letter |

Do not evaluate the campaign until day 60. Most direct-mail responses land on touch two or
three; pulling the plug after touch one is the standard way to conclude, wrongly, that direct
mail doesn't work.

**Phone follow-up** is legal for sole traders (CTPS-screen the list first) and is the highest-
converting step available. If Nick decides to use it, place it at day +7, between touches 1 and 2.
Deferred for now — no number to give out.

## Pre-build (Tier 1) variant

For the 5–8 prospects whose site gets built *before* posting, replace the offer paragraph in
Touch 1 with:

> **So here's the thing. I've already built it.**
>
> It's sitting at the code on the back of this letter, with your business name on it. Nobody
> else has seen it. Go and look — that's the whole ask, and it costs you nothing but the scan.
>
> If you like it, tell me what it was worth to you and that's what you pay. If you don't,
> nothing happens and I'll take it down. I built it before asking because I'd rather show you
> than describe it to you, and because you've no reason yet to take a stranger's word for
> anything.
