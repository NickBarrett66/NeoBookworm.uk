Before you ask

The questions I'd ask, in your shoes.

I've already got a Facebook page — why do I need a website too?

Facebook's good for photos and quick updates, but you don't own that page — Facebook does, and its algorithm decides who sees your posts. A website is yours, it's what turns up when someone Googles "tyre fitting near [your town]" rather than searches Facebook, and it still works for the customers who don't have an account. Most fitters end up running both — the website's usually what a stranger finds first.

What happens if I want to cancel?

No exit fee either way, just a month's notice. For The Site, that's simple — the pages, words and photos are sent over and your web address is transferred into your name if I registered it. Booking is different: it runs on a calendar connection I host, so that switches off when you cancel rather than transferring as files. Your site itself keeps working; you just lose that extra machinery.

How long does this take?

I build a first version from what I can find about your business, usually within a few days of you getting in touch. You look it over and tell me what to change, and we go back and forth until it's right — there's no meeting to book first — just a short form with a few details to get started.

What do you actually need from me?

To get started: your business name and town. I'll see what I can find online for a first draft — but tyre fitters aren't always easy to research from scratch, especially if you're not on Google or Facebook much. If there's not enough out there, I'll come back with a couple of quick questions rather than guess — what you do, what you don't, roughly what you charge. Photos of your van, your bay or your work help too, but nothing's required just to get going.

I'm not techy — will I be able to keep it going?

Yes. Once it's live you don't need to touch anything — it just sits there working. If you ever want something changed, you email me and I do it; you're not learning software or logging into a dashboard. That's what the monthly's for.
