The website build

The price for your build is agreed with you before any work begins, and it covers the design, build, and launch of your new website. Depending on the plan you choose, that can range from a simple starter site offered on a "pay what you think it's worth" basis up to a fixed-price build — you'll always know the exact figure, or the basis for it, before anything is commissioned. Every build includes:

  • A site built around your business and how you work
  • Your services, contact details, and the area you cover
  • A design that looks right on phones, tablets, and desktop
  • Setup on hosting so it is ready for people to find
  • Your web address (e.g. yourbusiness.co.uk) — most cost £10–15/year and that's included once you've confirmed you want to go ahead. While you're reviewing the site it sits on a temporary address, and your own web address is registered in your name once your build payment is made. If your preferred name is taken I'll suggest alternatives at no extra cost
  • Changes included until you're happy — you review the site before it goes live and I make any adjustments you need

You see it before you pay. I'll show you the finished site before asking for anything. If it's not right for you, there's no obligation to proceed and the site won't go live.

The build fee is a single payment, made once you've approved the site and before it goes live. Once it's paid the site goes live and it's yours outright — including your web address, registered in your name — with no hidden costs. Where a site is offered on a "pay what you think it's worth" basis, keeping it live includes a monthly plan from the start; that's how a name-your-price build is made to work. A fixed-price build carries no ongoing obligation unless you choose to add a monthly plan.

Monthly plans (optional)

From £9.99 / month

For a fixed-price build a monthly plan is entirely your choice — your site stays live either way. For a "pay what you think it's worth" build a monthly plan is included from the start, which is what lets the site be offered on that basis. Plans come in tiers so you only pay for what you need: the base plan keeps your site live and looked after, and higher tiers add features such as online booking, appointment reminders, and vehicle or address look-ups. The exact inclusions and price are those shown to you when you sign up.

Every plan covers, as a minimum:

  • Keeping your site live, fast, and on the right hosting
  • Security and software updates as they come out
  • Small text and content changes (up to around 30 minutes of work a month)
  • Your annual web address renewal — I sort it so it stays registered in your name (typically £8–15/year to the registrar, covered while you're on a plan)

Bigger changes — new pages, new sections, new features — are quoted separately at £25 an hour. I will always tell you the cost before starting.

You can cancel at any time with no fuss. Cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing month, and your site files are yours regardless.

The monthly price shown is the rate at the time you sign up. I will keep this as stable as I can — any increases would be driven by rises in the costs involved in running your site, or by changes to what the plan includes. If I do need to change the price, I will give you at least 30 days' notice by email before it takes effect, and you are free to cancel with no penalty if you would prefer not to continue at the new rate.

Booking, reminders and look-ups

If your plan includes online booking, appointment reminders (by email or text), or vehicle and address look-ups, the following also applies:

  • Third-party services. These features rely on outside providers — for example Google Calendar for bookings, an SMS provider for texts, and the DVLA vehicle data service and Postcoder for look-ups. I set them up and keep them running, but I can't guarantee a third party will never have an outage or change its service.
  • Included allowances. Higher tiers include a monthly allowance of look-ups and text messages, so your bill stays predictable. If you regularly go beyond your allowance I'll talk to you about moving to a plan that fits — I won't spring surprise charges on you.
  • Your customers' data. When your customers book through your site, that booking information belongs to you — you are the data controller and I act as your data processor, handling it only to run the booking service on your behalf and keeping it secure. I don't use your customers' details for anything else.
