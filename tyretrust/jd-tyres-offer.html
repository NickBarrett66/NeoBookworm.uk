The questions I'd ask, in your shoes

Why free? What's the catch?

No catch — I specialise in websites for tyre fitters, and yours is one of the first I've built. I'd rather you had a working site to judge than a sales pitch. The only thing that isn't free is keeping it live once you're happy — that's the £9.99/mo.

I've already got a website (Google Sites) — why would I switch?

You've already taken the step most fitters haven't — getting online at all. This one's built specifically around your tyre business: your reviews, your brands, your coverage area, clear directions to your unit, fast on a phone, and set up so it turns up on Google searches. No pressure either way — have a look and decide for yourself.

Who owns it — and what happens to the web address?

You do — the site and the web address. Right now it's sitting on a temporary web address just so you can look it over, so there's nothing to lose if it's not for you. Once you say yes, it moves to its own proper web address registered in your name, and the pages, words and photos are always yours to keep.

How do I actually pay the £9.99/mo?

Once you're happy and ready to go live, I'll send a single secure card link that sets up the monthly payment — no invoices to remember, nothing paid by hand. Nothing is taken until you say yes, and the site only goes live once that's set up.

What if I want changes?

Just email me — small wording or photo changes are usually 15–30 minutes and covered as part of getting things right early on. For anything bigger — new pages, new features, a proper rethink of a section — I'll quote it at £25 an hour before I start, so you always know the cost upfront. Either way, you're not logging into a dashboard or learning software.

What if I want to cancel?

No exit fee, just a month's notice. You can revert to your previous site — or anything you wish.
