Privacy Policy

TyreTrust contacts local tyre businesses using publicly available information. I hold only the minimum data needed to reach you, I never share it, and you can opt out at any time with a single reply.

Last updated: 22 July 2026
TyreTrust — a trading name of NeoBookworm, run by Nick Barrett, Swindon.
Any question about this policy: use the form at the bottom of this page, or email nick@tyretrust.uk directly.

1. Who I am

This website is operated by Nick Barrett, trading as TyreTrust — a trading name of NeoBookworm, my website-building business for UK tradespeople. TyreTrust isn't a separate company; I am the data controller for the information described in this policy.

For any privacy-related query, use the form below.

2. Data I collect when I contact you

When I get in touch with a tyre business — by letter, postcard, phone or email — I collect only the following information, sourced exclusively from publicly available business listings such as Google Maps and Facebook business pages:

  • Business or trading name
  • Personal name (where a contact name is shown on the listing)
  • Business email address
  • Business phone number
  • Business postal address (where shown)
  • Approximate location or postcode

I do not collect any sensitive personal data. Where I contact a business by post, I may follow up once by phone on the publicly listed business number — see "Why I contact you" below. I screen business numbers against the Corporate Telephone Preference Service (CTPS) before making any such call.

3. Information you give me directly

When you get in touch or ask me to build your site, you give me information yourself. This includes:

  • The enquiry form on this site — your name, business name, town, what you do (depot, mobile, or both), and email address, plus anything you write, so I can build your site and reply to you.
  • Anything you send afterwards — photos, logos or details you email me to help build the finished site.

I use this information only to respond to you and to build and run the website you've asked for. I keep it for as long as we're working together and in line with the retention section below.

4. Booking, reminder and job-board demos

The live booking, reminder-email and job-board demos on this site are for illustration only — trying them doesn't create a real booking, and nothing you enter into a demo is stored, emailed or sent anywhere. Any reg-plate or tyre-size look-up shown in a demo uses sample data, clearly marked as such.

If I build a website for your business that includes real online booking, that site handles its own customers' real data and has its own privacy policy covering it — your business is the data controller for those bookings, and I act only as your data processor.

5. Cookies and analytics

You can review or change your cookie preferences at any time: Manage cookie preferences

This site uses an essential cookie to remember your cookie choices. If you accept analytics cookies, it also uses Google Analytics to understand which pages are most useful. Analytics is opt-in — it stays off unless you choose to enable it.

6. Why I contact you

My outreach is conducted on the following lawful bases under UK data protection law:

  • UK GDPR — Legitimate Interests (Article 6(1)(f)): for contacts at limited companies and other corporate entities, I rely on legitimate interests to make a proportionate, relevant business-to-business approach.
  • PECR — Corporate subscribers: where the recipient is a corporate body (e.g. a Ltd company), direct marketing by electronic means is permitted under Regulation 22 of PECR without prior consent, provided an opt-out is clearly offered in every message.

For confirmed limited companies, I may send an initial email and a small number of follow-up emails or letters if there has been no response. I will never contact any business again once an opt-out request has been received.

Sole traders and non-limited partnerships: I never send these businesses cold marketing emails — they are treated as individual subscribers under PECR and require prior consent. They may receive a physical letter by post, and I may follow up once by phone on the publicly listed business number (screened against the CTPS first). A request to stop, by any method, is honoured immediately.

7. How to opt out

You can opt out at any time using either method below:

  • Reply to my email with the word "unsubscribe", or tell me by post or phone that you'd rather not be contacted
  • Use the form below and ask to be removed

I will remove your details from my records immediately and will not contact you again. I retain only a minimal suppression record (the email address alone) to ensure I do not inadvertently re-contact you in future.

8. Who processes data on my behalf

I never sell, rent, or trade your information, and I don't share it for anyone else's marketing. I do use a small number of trusted service providers who process data on my behalf so the service can run:

  • Cloudflare — website hosting and handling the enquiry form on this site
  • Google — website analytics (opt-in only)
  • Apple (iCloud) — delivers the email notification when someone submits the enquiry form
  • Stripe — secure card payments, only once you decide to go ahead (I never see or store your card details)

Each of these acts only on my instructions and is bound to keep the data secure. I may add or change providers as the service evolves, and I'll keep this list up to date.

9. Data retention

Outreach contact records (from letters, postcards or emails) are retained for up to 12 months from the date of last contact, after which all personal data fields are deleted. I retain only a minimal suppression record (business name, contact status, and date of first contact) indefinitely — solely to ensure I do not inadvertently re-contact a business that has previously opted out or been closed.

Information you give me directly — through the enquiry form, or by email once we're talking — is kept for as long as we're working together and for a reasonable period afterwards, then deleted.

10. Your rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

Access

Ask for a copy of any personal information I hold about you.

Correction

Ask me to correct anything that's wrong or out of date.

Deletion

Ask me to delete your information where I no longer need it.

Objection

Object to me using your information where the legal basis is legitimate interests. I'll stop unless there's a compelling reason to continue.

To exercise any of these rights, use the form below. If your query is about a booking you made on another business's website, that business is the controller — I'll pass your request to them or help you reach them. You can also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at any time.

11. Changes to this policy

If I make material changes to this policy, I'll update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. I recommend checking back periodically if you have an ongoing interest in how I handle data.
