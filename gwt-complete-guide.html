Great Western Trail

Complete Interactive Guide · Base Game (1st Edition)

🔄 Every Turn — 3 Phases

On your turn carry out three phases in this order, then play passes left. Your cattleman travels the trail from the lower-right toward Kansas City, reaching it about 5–7 times per game.

A

Move your cattleman

Move forward to another location. At least 1 step, up to your step limit (starts at 3 or 4).

⚠️ Only tiles are locations — building, hazard, teepee tiles, and Kansas City. Empty spaces are NOT locations and cost no step. You can never pass over Kansas City.
✋ Moving to or past a tile with a green or black hand costs a fee — to the bank for hazards/teepees, to the owner for their private building. You must pay what you can, but you may still move even if you can't pay in full. Fees can never be paid retroactively.
Player count🟢 Green hand⬛ Black hand
2 players$2$2
3 players$2$1
4 players$1$2
B

Use the location's action(s)

What you can do depends entirely on where you stopped. Own/neutral buildings give local actions; elsewhere you take a single auxiliary action; Kansas City runs 5 subphases.

C

Draw back to your hand limit

Draw until you hold your hand-limit cards (starts at 4, up to 6). Shuffle discard pile into draw stack only when you must draw and have none left.

💡 First turn only: place your cattleman on any neutral building and go straight to phase B.
🎯 Plan your landing before you move. Work backwards from where you want to be — only on own/neutral buildings can you take local actions.

📍 Where Did You Land?

Phase B options depend entirely on the tile you stopped on.

🏚️ Neutral building (A–G)

Use either the tile's local actions (each once, any order) or one single auxiliary action. You cannot do both.

🏠 Your own building

Same as neutral — use its local actions free of charge. This is why you place buildings where you'll naturally land.

🏛️ Opponent's building

You already paid the hand fee on arrival. Here you may only take one single auxiliary action — not the building's actions.

⚠️ Hazard / 🪶 Teepee

Pay any hand fee on arrival. Then take one single auxiliary action only.

🏙️ Kansas City

Run all 5 subphases in order — Foresight ×3, Income, Delivery.

🏙️ Kansas City — 5 Subphases

When your cattleman reaches Kansas City, carry out all five subphases in order along the numbered spots.

1–3

Foresight (×3)

At each of the 3 foresight spots, choose 1 of the 2 tiles shown and place it in its matching section — worker to job market, teepee to Indian trade section, hazard to its hazard section.

⚠️ When a worker fills the job market's last spot in a row, the job market token advances. Along a yellow arrow → refill the cattle market. Along the final red arrow → game end triggered.
4

Income

Reveal your whole hand. Sum the breeding value of each different cattle type — each type scores once only, no matter how many copies. Optionally add certificates. Take that many dollars, then discard your hand.

⚠️ Most-misplayed rule: duplicates score nothing. Dutch Belt 2 + Dutch Belt 2 + Brown Swiss 3 + Holstein 3 = 3 types = $8, not $10.
💡 Certificates bridge income gaps. Temporary ones (on your certificate track, limit starts at 3) are spent by moving your marker backwards at income — earn them back during the trail with forward actions. Permanent ones (top half of station master tiles) add +1 forever automatically. Save temporaries for a delivery that crosses $10 or $12.
5

Delivery

Choose a city whose value ≤ your total breeding value, that you haven't delivered to before (except Kansas City & San Francisco, which allow repeat deliveries). Place a disc, unlocking that disc space's ability. Pay $1 transport per cross between your engine's nose and the city.

🎯 Clearing hand-limit disc spaces costs $5 and requires $10/$12 income. Clearing step-limit spaces gives you $3 and earns 3 VP. Plan which spaces to clear in what order — it's a major part of your engine.

After Delivery: move your cattleman back to the horseman start space (lower-right corner), then refill the 3 empty foresight spaces with tiles matching each spot's back number.

  • Any unused cowboy may instead draw 2 cards from the market cattle stack and add them face up to the cattle market (refreshes market variety).
  • You can only buy cards currently available in the cattle market.
  • All acquired cards go face up onto your personal discard pile (not your hand).
  • You may always buy fewer cards than your cowboys allow.

💡 Buy cattle just after your deck reshuffles — the card lands in the fresh draw stack and reaches your hand sooner.

👷 Hire One Worker

Choose exactly one worker tile from the job market, pay its hiring cost, place it on the leftmost free space in its row on your player board.

  • You cannot hire from the row where the job market token currently sits — those workers are frozen until the token moves on.
  • Base hiring cost = printed on the right side of the worker's row. The action icon can modify this: = no change · +$2 more · −$1 cheaper.
  • You start with 1 pre-placed worker of each type, so the first hire fills space 2. Place left → right.
  • If the space you land on shows an immediate action, perform it now or forfeit — you cannot use it later.
  • If a row is already full, you cannot hire another worker of that type.
  • Workers placed on the 5th or 6th space are worth 4 VP each at game end.
⚠️ Most-missed rule: you can never hire from the row with the job market token — even if it's the only row with workers you want. Check the token position before choosing.
🎯 Hiring a second engineer early unlocks many private tile actions and lets you reach far cities faster. Cowboys also let you refill the cattle market as a bonus — useful even on a builder path.

🏗️ Place a Private Building Tile

Option A — Place on an empty building space on the trail:

  • You need craftsmen in your row ≥ the number shown in the tile's top-left corner.
  • Cost: $2 for each craftsman required (e.g. tile requiring 3 = $6).
  • Exception: if using the craftsman-row immediate actions, cost drops to $1 per craftsman.

Option B — Replace one of your own tiles already on the trail:

  • The replacement tile must have a higher craftsman requirement than the existing one.
  • You only need the difference in craftsmen and pay only the difference in cost.
  • The old tile is permanently removed from the game — returned to the box.

The woods: a tile is "in the woods" if at least one of its edges touches the woods area printed on the board. Tiles 1a and 7b reward buildings in the woods.

⚠️ The building tile side is identical for ALL players in a given game and cannot be flipped. Decide sides at setup and stick with them.
💡 The only other way to place buildings is via craftsman immediate actions — these cost $1 per craftsman rather than $2, making them significantly cheaper than building tile B.

🚂 Move Your Engine Forward

Move up to as many spaces forward as you have engineers in your row (or the fixed number shown on the icon).

  • No two engines share a space (starting space is the exception). Skip over occupied spaces as if they don't exist — they don't count toward your movement.
  • You may move fewer spaces than allowed — but once you stop, you forfeit remaining movement from that action.
  • Turnout spaces branch off numbered spaces — each turnout counts as one additional space between the two spaces it connects. You can stop on a turnout to upgrade its station.

↩️ End-of-Track Rule (Space 39)

  • If your engine reaches space 39, movement ends there.
  • You must then move your engine backwards — at least 1 space, to any free space (may include a turnout).
  • Immediately gain $3.
  • If you stop on a turnout, you may upgrade that station as usual.

Moving backwards (from any action): skip occupied spaces just like forward movement. You can stop on a turnout. You cannot move fewer spaces backwards than the action requires.

If your engine is on the starting space, you cannot use any action that requires backwards movement.

🏚️ Upgrading a Train Station

When your engine stops on a turnout space, you may immediately choose to upgrade that train station.

  • You can only upgrade each station once (you already have a disc there). Other players' discs don't block you.
  • Pay the upgrade cost shown. Place one of your discs onto the station space — white-corner disc goes anywhere; dark-corner disc only on dark-corner station spaces.
  • If a station master tile sits next to the station, you may claim it: take the rightmost worker from any row on your player board, place it on the station master space, then take the station master tile face up beside your board.
  • This is your only chance to claim that tile — you cannot return for it later.
  • Removing a worker uncovers its immediate action; the next worker placed there can trigger it again.

Station master tiles: upper half = immediate action (perform or forfeit) or permanent certificate (+1 to breeding value forever). Lower half = unique end-game VP condition.

⚠️ The extraordinary delivery action (on private tile 9a) lets you move backwards and deliver to a city equal to spaces moved — with no transport costs. The city value must be ≤ the number of spaces moved (occupied and turnout spaces don't count).

📜 Playing an Objective Card

When you have an objective card in hand during your turn, you may play it at these moments only:

  • Before phase A (before moving your cattleman), or
  • Before or after any single action in phase B — but not in the middle of an action, and not once phase C has begun.

To play: place it face up in your personal objective area below your board, then perform its immediate action (top-left) or forfeit it.

Immediate actions on objective cards:

  • Move cattleman up to 3 steps forward (ignoring hand fees). Cannot move to Kansas City this way. If used before phase A, that location becomes your starting point for normal movement. If used after a phase-B action, you skip to phase C from the new location.
  • Draw up to 3 cards from your draw stack, then discard the same number.
  • Gain $2.
  • Move your engine up to 2 or 3 spaces forward.
  • Perform one single or double auxiliary action.

📊 Scoring Objective Cards at Game End

  • Each card lists tasks (e.g. have 1 building on board, have 1 blue teepee, etc.).
  • All tasks fulfilled → score the positive VP on the card.
  • Any task unfulfilled → subtract the negative VP on the card.
  • Critical: each fulfilled task counts toward only one card. Two cards both needing "1 blue teepee" require 2 blue teepees.
  • Cards still in your deck at game end: choose to score them or remove them from the game (no immediate action used).
  • Cards in your personal objective area: must be scored — no choice.
  • Your starting objective card has no negative VP value.

Possible tasks: private building tile on board · green teepee · blue teepee · any hazard tile · disc on a train station · cattle card with breeding value 3 in deck · West Highland in deck · Texas Longhorn in deck · disc on San Francisco.

💡 Keeping objective cards in your deck gives you a choice at game end — you can bin unachievable ones. Once placed in your objective area, you're committed to trying.

🟤 Clearing Disc Spaces — What Each One Does

When you deliver to a city and place a disc on its crest, you clear that disc space on your board, unlocking its ability. Some spaces have costs or rewards:

Hand limit+1 to hand limit (4 → 5 → 6)Pay $5 immediately (must have it)

⬜⬛ White Corner vs. Dark Corner Discs

  • White-corner disc spaces on your board: disc may go on any city or station space.
  • Dark-corner disc spaces on your board: disc may only go on city / station spaces that also have dark corners.
  • Special case: if you need to place a white-corner disc but only have dark-corner discs remaining, you may use a dark-corner disc on a white-corner space.
  • If you have no discs at all, remove one of your discs from any train station and use that one.

🏁 Triggering the End of the Game

The game ends when a worker tile fills the last space of the job market during Kansas City subphases 2 or 3, causing the job market token to move along the red arrow.

  • The active player takes the job market token and places it in front of them (2 VP at scoring).
  • If this happened in subphase 2, they carry out subphase 3 normally — but can't choose another worker tile (no space left). If only workers remain in foresight spots 3, skip subphase 3 entirely.
  • They finish subphases 4 (Income) and 5 (Delivery) as normal, refill foresight spaces, and that was their last turn.

🔄 The Final Round

Each other player takes one final turn in clockwise order.

  • They move their cattleman and use the reached location as usual.
  • Players reaching Kansas City in their final turn carry out all 5 subphases — but during subphases 2 and 3, they cannot choose worker tiles from foresight spaces (skip if only workers remain).
  • Every player must still refill empty foresight spaces at the end of their final turn.

After the last player finishes, proceed to Final Scoring.

⚠️ The game end can arrive suddenly in the last 2–3 rows of the job market. If you see the token approaching the red arrow, stop building and start banking VP.

🎯 Strategy & Style of Play

GWT is a game of compound growth. Each loop of the trail should be worth more than the last. The winner builds the most efficient engine and reads the game's pace.

Your First Game — Simple Priorities

Ignore nothing, but focus on these in order. Use a-sides only for all building tiles in your first game — they are simpler and more consistent.

1
Get a 5th colour before anything else. Your starting 14 cards have only 4 breeds (value max = 7). One market cattle card in a new colour (any) jumps you to $10 income. Target 1 cowboy purchase early.
2
Target $10 income as your first milestone — it funds clearing the first hand/step disc spaces, which unlocks real engine growth. Use your Certificate Marker to bridge the last $1–2.
3
Push your engine at least to the 3rd city group by mid-game. Transport costs eat into income if your train stays near the start. Two engineers hired early pay for themselves quickly.
4
Place 1–2 buildings early on your natural route. Even one building lets you land on it repeatedly instead of paying hand fees or wasting a turn on a forced auxiliary action.
5
Watch the job market token. When it enters the last 2–3 rows, stop spending money on workers and start making every delivery count. The game can end before you expect it.
💡 Don't try to do everything in your first game. Pick one of the three engines (cowboys / engineers / builders) and lean into it. A focused build always outperforms a spread-thin one.

Opening priorities

💡 Buy cattle before raising your hand limit. A 5th hand slot is wasted if you only have 4 distinct colours. New colour first, hand expansion second.
💡 Of equal-cost cattle, buy the one with more VP (Ayrshire > Brown Swiss > Holstein at value 3). New colours boost income immediately; duplicates add nothing at delivery.
💡 Target $10 income as your first milestone — it unlocks the first hand/disc ability and starts the snowball. Use certificates to bridge the final $1–2.
💡 Buy cattle just after your deck reshuffles, not just before. The card then sits in the fresh draw stack and reaches your hand sooner.

Reading the pace

🎯 Every delivery ticks the job-market clock. A fast player reaching Kansas City often controls when the game ends — finishing it before a slow engine matures. Watch the job-market token: in its last 2–3 rows, stop building and start cashing in for VP.
🎯 Buildings in the woods: several tiles (1a, 7b) reward buildings touching the woods edge. If committing to builder, the woods bonuses compound meaningfully.

Hazard tiles: sum of VP on each collected hazard tile.

6

Cattle VP: sum of VP on all cattle cards in your entire deck.

7

Objective cards: fulfilled cards score positive VP; unfulfilled ones in your objective area subtract their negative value.

8

Station master tasks: VP from the individual tasks on station master tiles you hold.

9

Workers: 4 VP for each worker placed on the 5th or 6th space of any row in your worker section. (Workers on spaces 1–4 score nothing from this category — but some station master tiles separately award 1 VP per worker.)

10

Disc space: 3 VP if you cleared the special step-limit disc space.

11

Job market token: 2 VP if you hold it (you triggered game end).

💡 Beginners routinely undercount workers (cat. 9), forget hazard tile VP, and skip converting money. Run through all 11 before totalling.

📋 Setup Checklist

Tap any step to mark it done. Takes about 10 minutes once you know the game.

1
Place the game board in the middle of the table.
2
Shuffle 5 station master tiles; place 1 face up on each of the 5 station master spaces on the board.
3
Place the 7 neutral buildings on spaces A–G (first game: fixed positions A→G; later games: shuffle and place randomly).
4
Place the job market token on the top dashed space of the job market (above row 1).
5
Sort the worker / teepee / hazard tiles by back number (1 / 2 / 3) into 3 face-down stacks per type. Shuffle each stack. These are your 3 Kansas City supply piles.
6
Reveal back-1 tiles: teepee tiles go into the Indian trade section in ascending value order (cheapest first, bottom-left). Hazard tiles go to their matching section. 7 tiles total placed.
7
Reveal back-2 (worker) tiles into the job market: start from your player-count sign, fill left to right, up to and including the job market token row.
8
Fill the 6 foresight spaces: place 2 back-1 tiles on spots 1, 2 back-2 tiles on spots 2, and 2 back-3 tiles on spots 3 (face up).
9
Shuffle the 36 market cattle cards. Deal 7 / 10 / 13 cards (for 2 / 3 / 4 players) face up into the cattle market in 5 colour columns: yellow → red → blue → brown → purple.
10
Shuffle the 24 objective cards. Reveal 4 as the face-up general objective display. Leave the rest as a face-down stack.
11
Place all coins in a central bank. The bank is unlimited — use extra counters if needed.
12
Each player takes their player board and (in 3–4 player games) the player-count tile covering Phase A.
13
Each player arranges their 10 private building tiles a-side up in ascending number order above their player board.
14
Each player takes 14 discs and covers all the coloured spaces on their player board (leave the 2 grey spaces uncovered). Places 1 engine on the red starting space, 1 certificate marker on 0, and 1 cattleman at the start of the trail.
15
Each player shuffles their 14 starting cattle cards (5 Jersey + 3 each of Dutch Belt, Black Angus, Guernsey) face down as their personal draw stack. Draw 4 cards to hand.
16
Deal each player 1 random objective card face up. This is their starting objective — placed in front of them (objective area), not in hand.
17
Determine start player. They receive $6; the next player clockwise gets $7; then $8; then $9 (4-player game).
