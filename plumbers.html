Why it matters

When a pipe bursts at 10pm, your customer decides in minutes — not days.

Most plumbing calls start with a search. A boiler that’s stopped working, a radiator that won’t heat up, a leak that’s getting worse. The person searching is usually stressed, sometimes standing in a puddle, and they’re not going to spend half an hour comparing options. They’ll look at two or three plumbers and call the one that looks right.

That’s the moment a website earns its keep. Not because it’s flashy — because it loads fast on a phone, it shows your Gas Safe number, it says clearly where you work and what you do, and it has a number they can tap to call you straight away. That’s all it takes to be the one they ring instead of the next result down.

It works for bigger jobs too. Someone looking at a bathroom refit or a full heating system will spend more time choosing — and that’s where photos of previous work, a clear list of services, and a few genuine reviews tip the balance. A website gives you somewhere to put all of that in one place, instead of hoping they find your Facebook page and scroll far enough.

And unlike Checkatrade or Bark, a website brings you direct enquiries. No commission, no competing quotes, no racing to reply first. Someone visits your site, decides they want you, and gets in touch. That’s a better kind of job to win.
